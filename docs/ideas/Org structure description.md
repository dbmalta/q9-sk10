The most basic grouping is a section. the section defines the age-group (e.g. Beavers 5-7 yrs, Cubs 9-11, Scouts 11-14, Venures 14-18 etc.) The names of these secitons and the actual age groups can be defined by the user. 

One or more sections will form a Scout Group/Unit. Some groups may have only one section, while others may have multiple sections.

A number of Scout Groups/Units will form a District. A District is a geographical area that may contain several Scout Groups/Units.

A number of Districts will form a County. A County is a larger geographical area that may contain several Districts. Sometimes this is also called a Region.

Districts, Counties/Regions are optional. Some Scout Groups/Units may not be part of a District or County/Region, depending on the size and structure of the organization. They may also have different names in different countries or organizations.

All of the scout units in a country will form the National Organization. The National Organization is responsible for overseeing and coordinating the activities of all the Scout Groups/Units in the country. In WOSM there can only be one National Organization per country.

There are some cases where several scout associations in a country may come together to form a National Organization, in these cases the National Organization is a federation of the different associations.

At the national level, you can have both voluntyeers and staff members. Staff members are paid employees of the National Organization, while volunteers are individuals who contribute their time and skills.

An NSO will always have:
- the National Board - a team of people elected to this position and led by the Persident/Chairperson/chief scout (depending on the setup.
- the executive branch, typically headed by the chief executive, chier commissioner or secretary general, and consisting of paid staff members or volunteer commissioners who are responsible for the day-to-day operations of the organization.
- supporting teams made up of staff or volunteers (or a mix) that may be of a permananet nature (e.g. a finance team) or temporary (e.g. a team to organize a specific event).

Important: a person could hold several roles at the same time and be part of more than one team at the same time. For example, a person could be a volunteer leader in a local Scout Group/Unit, while also being a member of the National Board and part of a supporting team at the national level. The roles and responsibilities of each position may vary depending on the specific organization and its structure.
